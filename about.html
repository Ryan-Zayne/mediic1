About Iyi-Enu Mission Hospital

University On The Niger Iyi-Enu Teaching Hospital Anambra State, established in 1907 by the Church Missionary Society, is one of Nigeria's oldest and most trusted mission hospitals. Located in Ogidi, Anambra State, we are committed to delivering compassionate, holistic, and high-quality healthcare while training the next generation of medical professionals.

The name "Iyi-Enu," meaning "Mouth of the Spring," reflects the life-giving mission of the hospitalâ€”a place of healing and hope for all. Founded on the Christian principle to "Preach and Heal," Iyi-Enu was designed not only to provide medical care but also to address the spiritual and emotional needs of its patients.

OUR HISTORY

A Legacy of Faith and Healing Since 1907

Established in 1907 by the Church Missionary Society (CMS), Iyi-Enu Mission Hospital is one of Nigeria's oldest and most reputable mission hospitals. From its humble beginnings as a dispensary in Ozalla, Onitsha, it was later relocated to Iyi-Enu, Ogidi, along the Onitshaâ€“Enugu Expressway.

Pioneering Excellence in Healthcare and Education

Iyi-Enu Mission Hospital holds a unique place in the history of healthcare in Nigeria. It was the first hospital in southeastern Nigeria, and over the decades, it has achieved several pioneering milestones:

  • First in Nigeria to graduate trained midwives through its pioneering School of Midwifery
  • One of the earliest hospitals in the country to train qualified nurses
  • Established village maternity homes and introduced mobile medical services
  • Played a leading role in the early treatment and eradication of infectious diseases
  • Among the first hospitals in Nigeria to implement traveling doctors and mobile clinics

A Teaching and Referral Center

Today, Iyi-Enu has evolved into a full-fledged teaching and referral hospital, affiliated with the University on the Niger. It now serves as the University on the Niger Teaching Hospital, Iyi-Enu, and continues to be a vital hub for clinical education, research, and healthcare delivery.

The hospital proudly hosts:

  • The School of Nursing Iyi-Enu, one of Nigeria's oldest and most respected institutions
  • The School of Midwifery, renowned for producing highly competent midwives
  • The Lasbery College of Health Technology, offering diploma and certificate programs

SUSTAINING THE VISION

Faith-Based Healthcare Excellence

Guided by its founding mission and values, Iyi-Enu remains steadfast in its commitment to faith-based, affordable, and accessible healthcare delivery. The hospital is owned and managed by the Diocese on the Niger, Anglican Communion, and continues to serve as a symbol of integrity, medical excellence, and Christian compassion.

Through outreach programs, free medical missions, health education campaigns, and ongoing professional training, Iyi-Enu Mission Hospital continues to positively impact lives across generations and geographies. OUR ACHIEVEMENTS

Pioneering Healthcare Milestones

Over 115 years of continuous service, Iyi-Enu Mission Hospital has achieved numerous pioneering milestones in healthcare delivery and medical education in Nigeria.

First Hospital in Southeastern Nigeria

1907

Established as the first hospital in southeastern Nigeria by the Church Missionary Society, setting the foundation for healthcare in the region.

Pioneering School of Midwifery

Early 1900s

First in Nigeria to graduate trained midwives through its pioneering School of Midwifery, revolutionizing maternal care.

Mobile Medical Services

Mid-1900s

Among the first hospitals in Nigeria to implement traveling doctors and mobile clinics, decades before mobile health became a global trend.

University Teaching Hospital

Present

Evolved into a full-fledged teaching and referral hospital, affiliated with the University on the Niger, serving as a vital hub for clinical education.
